What Can You Do with an Economics Degree?

Economics offers versatile career opportunities, reflected in the diverse electives within the major. The Department has grouped electives into Econ Tracks, each aligning with specific career paths. Though these tracks don't appear on transcripts or diplomas, they can help guide your graduation plan.

The tracks are grouped based on similar skills that you develop in the econ major that are used within those careers. Students are welcome to take any elective, even if it spans multiple tracks.

💰

Banking and Finance

Prepares students for careers in financial services, corporate finance, and investment roles.

Common Roles

  • Investment Banking Analyst
  • Equity Research Analyst
  • Financial Analyst (FP&A)
  • Acquisition Specialist
  • Wealth Advisor
  • Risk and Pricing Analyst

Top Employers

JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, Nike, Adobe, American Airlines

Key Responsibilities

  • Building financial models for investments, mergers, and acquisitions
  • Analyzing market trends and company performance
  • Preparing reports and presentations for clients
  • Managing financial risk or portfolio strategy

Recommended Courses

ACC 201, ACC 202, FIN 201, IS 110, IS 201, ECON 450

Get Involved

Finance Club for networking and hands-on experience

Graduate Paths

MFA, MBA

Economic Consulting

Equips students with analytical and empirical tools for data-intensive roles at the intersection of economics, law, and public policy.

Common Roles

  • Analyst
  • Economic Consultant
  • Research Associate
  • Litigation Support Analyst

Top Employers

Analysis Group, Bates White, Cornerstone, Secretariat, NERA, Brookings Institution, American Enterprise Institute

Key Responsibilities

  • Apply econometric and statistical techniques to analyze complex datasets
  • Support expert witness testimony
  • Estimate damages or market effects in litigation contexts
  • Summarize findings in professional reports and memos

Recommended Courses

ECON 420, ECON 450, ECON 442, ECON 476, ECON 588

Get Involved

Research assistant positions with faculty

Graduate Paths

PhD in Economics, MBA

Policy/Non-Profit

Prepares students for impactful careers in public service, policy research, and international development.

Common Roles

  • Policy Analyst
  • Research Assistant (Federal Reserve, think tanks)
  • Entry-level Economist (federal agencies)
  • Program Analyst (nonprofits)

Top Employers

U.S. Departments (Commerce, Labor, Defense), World Bank, local municipalities, Church of Jesus Christ affiliates, other nonprofits

Key Responsibilities

  • Analyze economic data to shape policy (labor, health, education, trade)
  • Conduct cost-benefit evaluations of public programs
  • Draft policy briefs
  • Provide econometric support to research initiatives

Recommended Courses

ECON 440–475, POLI 200, POLI 203, POLI 331, Political Science or Urban and Regional Planning Minor

Get Involved

Beyond BYU, Foreign Service Student Organization, Model UN

Graduate Paths

MPA, Master of Public Policy

Pre-PhD

Tailored for students considering doctoral study in economics, public policy, finance, or related quantitative fields.

Common Roles

  • Predoctoral Research Assistant (Predoc)
  • Research Analyst
  • Research Assistant at academic institutions, think tanks, or Federal Reserve

What You'll Do

  • Intensive, hands-on academic-style research
  • Data cleaning and literature reviews
  • Econometric analysis
  • Replication or extension of existing studies

Recommended Courses

ECON 580, ECON 581, ECON 582, ECON 588, Math Minor or Major

Get Involved

Undergraduate research assistant positions with faculty

Graduate Paths

PhD in Economics, Public Policy, Finance, or Operations Research

Law

Supports students preparing for legal careers by emphasizing logical reasoning, critical thinking, and analytical writing skills rooted in economic reasoning.

Why Economics for Law?

Economics majors are well-regarded by law schools for their rigorous training in logic, argumentation, and policy analysis. Success hinges on strong academic performance and a high LSAT score.

Recommended Courses

ECON 442 (Behavioral Economics), ECON 476 (Law and Economics), ECON 478 (Antitrust and Regulation)

Get Involved

Seek undergraduate research or internships exposing you to legal reasoning or public policy

Graduate Paths

JD (Law School)

Which Track is Best for Me?
